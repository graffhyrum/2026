## Developer Relations & Community Teams

### Who They Are
Developer Relations Managers, Community Managers, Developer Advocates, or anyone responsible for engaging with developer communities.

### Why They Care
Community engagement is literally their job. 
Python conferences provide direct access to their target audience and align perfectly with their KPIs around developer engagement, community visibility, and technical brand building.

### What They Control
Community budgets, event sponsorships, developer marketing funds.

### How to Find Them

Check your internal directory for the following titles:
- Developer Advocate
- Developer Relations
- DevRel
- Community Manager 

In smaller companies, this might be a Developer Advocate or Marketing team member.

## Handling Common Objections

When advocating for sponsorship internally, you may encounter pushback. Here's how to respond:

### "We don't have budget for this."

**Response:** "I understand budget is tight. Bronze tier is $1K less than the cost of a single job posting or a mid-level LinkedIn Recruiter license for a month. One quality hire or qualified lead could cover the cost 10x over. Also, they sold a sponsorship the first day if budget opens up later, the opportunity might be gone."

### "How do we know this is worth it?"

**Response:** "PyTexas has been running for 20 years it's the oldest regional Python conference in North America. The fact that a sponsor signed up day one of the prospectus shows market validation. Plus, past sponsors include cloud providers, dev tool companies, consulting firms, and AI/ML companies, so there's precedent for ROI."

### "We already sponsor PyCon US. Why another Python conference?"

**Response:** "PyCon US is national with 2,500+ attendees great for broad visibility. PyTexas is regional with 200-250 attendees, mostly Texas-based. It's more targeted, more intimate, and way more cost-effective. You're not competing for attention with 100 other sponsors. Plus, 83% of attendees are from Texas, so if we're hiring or selling here, it's hyper-relevant."

### "Python isn't our primary language/focus."

**Response:** "Python is the #1 most-used language globally and dominates in data science, ML, DevOps, and backend engineering. Even if it's not our primary language, these are engineers who work across stacks."

### "Can we do this next year?"

**Response:** "Absolutely, but this is the 20th anniversary year, which is likely to have higher attendance and visibility. Also, the prospectus just went out and a sponsor signed up the first day - popular tiers will sell out. If we wait, we might miss the opportunity altogether."

### "Who approved sponsorships last year?"

**Response:** "Great question let me find out! Do you know who I should talk to in [DevRel/Marketing/Recruiting]?" *(Use this to redirect to the right person.)*
