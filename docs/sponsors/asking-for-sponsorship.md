## Handling Common Objections

When advocating for sponsorship internally, you may encounter pushback. Here's how to respond:

### "We don't have budget for this."

**Response:** "I understand budget is tight. Bronze tier is $1Kless than the cost of a single job posting or a mid-level LinkedIn Recruiter license for a month. One quality hire or qualified lead could cover the cost 10x over. Also, they sold a sponsorship the first dayif budget opens up later, the opportunity might be gone."

### "How do we know this is worth it?"

**Response:** "PyTexas has been running for 20 yearsit's the oldest regional Python conference in North America. The fact that a sponsor signed up day one of the prospectus shows market validation. Plus, past sponsors include cloud providers, dev tool companies, consulting firms, and AI/ML companies, so there's precedent for ROI."

### "We already sponsor PyCon US. Why another Python conference?"

**Response:** "PyCon US is national with 2,500+ attendeesgreat for broad visibility. PyTexas is regional with 200-250 attendees, mostly Texas-based. It's more targeted, more intimate, and way more cost-effective. You're not competing for attention with 100 other sponsors. Plus, 83% of attendees are from Texas, so if we're hiring or selling here, it's hyper-relevant."

### "Python isn't our primary language/focus."

**Response:** "Python is the #2 most-used language globally and dominates in data science, ML, DevOps, and backend engineering. Even if it's not our primary language, these are engineers who work across stacks. Also, 9% of attendees are DevOps/SRE, 11% are data scientiststhese roles touch multiple technologies."

### "Can we do this next year?"

**Response:** "Absolutely, but this is the 20th anniversary year, which is likely to have higher attendance and visibility. Also, the prospectus just went out and a sponsor signed up the first daypopular tiers will sell out. If we wait, we might miss the opportunity altogether."

### "Who approved sponsorships last year?"

**Response:** "Great questionlet me find out! Do you know who I should talk to in [DevRel/Marketing/Recruiting]?" *(Use this to redirect to the right person.)*

## Tips for Internal Champions

### Frame It Around Their Goals

Match your message to what your audience cares about:

- **DevRel/Community:** "This hits our community engagement goals and gives us content for our blog/social."
- **Recruiting:** "This is a sourcing eventwe'd have direct access to 250 Python engineers in one place."
- **Marketing:** "This is a thought leadership play and brand awareness in the Texas market."
- **Engineering Leadership:** "This is a professional development investment + employer brand boost."
- **Sales:** "This is a lead-gen play with built-in engagement opportunities."

### Emphasize the 20th Anniversary

Milestone years get more attention, more press, and more nostalgia-driven attendance. Frame this as a unique opportunity that won't come around again.

### Create Urgency with Scarcity

- "They sold a sponsorship the first day. Only 15 Bronze spots, 5 Gold, 2 Platinum."
- "If we wait, we might be too late."

### Offer to Do the Work

Remove friction by making it easy for decision-makers:

- "I'm happy to pull together a one-pager for you to present internally."
- "I can set up the intro call with the PyTexas teamyou just need to show up."
- "I can draft the email for you to forward to the right team."

### Follow Up Persistently but Politely

Budget cycles are slow. Send a follow-up after 5-7 days: "Just wanted to bump thisI know PyTexas is filling up fast and didn't want us to miss the opportunity."

### Start with Someone You Know

If you have a good relationship with your engineering manager, start there. They can direct you to the right budget owner. Use the "I'm not asking for money from you" frameyou're asking them to *connect you* with the right person. This takes pressure off.

### Celebrate Small Wins

Even if they say "not this year," ask, "Who should I talk to next year?" Build the relationship now for future opportunities.
